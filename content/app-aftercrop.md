# Aftercrop

**A Konic Technologies Product — Case Study on Design and Development**

In India, medium-sized warehouses and cold storage are disorganised but vital for farmers and reducing food waste. Current tracking methods are outdated and complex. The goal was to create user-friendly, mobile-first software to simplify warehouse operations.

## User Research

### Interviews
Through user interviews, we explored experiences and frustrations of warehouse stakeholders. A common thread: existing solutions lacked emphasis on design and ease of use, especially on mobile devices.

### Ethnographic Studies
Dissected cluttered interfaces of current warehouse management systems. The findings highlighted a gap: a delightful, user-friendly solution was missing on both mobile and desktop.

### Competitive Analysis
Existing products had complex interfaces designed for desktop use. The market lacked a product that caters to both mobile and desktop users with equal proficiency.

## Key Findings

### Design and Usability
Existing products lack emphasis on design and ease of use. Software looks like it was designed for desktops by older generations — lots of functionality packed into clunky user interfaces. None works well on mobile.

**Realisation:** Scope for a product that is a delight to use — both on mobile and desktop.

### Adaptability
Every product tried to introduce several new workflows that warehouses are not familiar with, causing too much disruption and never reaching adaptability. Even customers who built in-house solutions ended up with staff unable to adapt because of interface complexity.

**Realisation:** Strong need for an easy-to-use solution that even unskilled staff can pick up quickly because it fits their workflow without disrupting.

### Availability
Some products only available as on-premise installations. Cloud-based solutions existed but were poorly implemented. Strong concern for data security.

**Realisation:** Potential for a modern cloud-based SaaS with strong focus on data security without vendor lock-in.

## Target Audience

Three user groups with varying literacy levels:

1. **Owners / Power Users** — Professional, comfortable with phones and computers. Need dashboard with summaries and reports.
2. **Managers** — Not good with computers, can use mobile phone. Track and keep accounts with pen and paper. Shifting to phone/computer can be disruptive unless extremely easy.
3. **Staff / Workers** — Can repeat a simple guided process. Own a phone but can't do more than input minimal information. Pages must be very simple.

## USP — Key Selling Points

1. **Design takes precedence** — Design and usability as the key differentiator
2. **Least disruption** — Process similar to current pen-and-paper workflows for quick onboarding
3. **Portability** — Mobile-first interfaces. Managers move around warehouses and need quick mobile access.

## The Solution

### Focus 1: Easy-to-use mobile-first design

- **Tables vs Lists and Cards** — Replaced all tables with cards and lists for mobile. Carefully designed each card with the right amount of information.
- **Feels like an app** — Elements similar to native mobile apps for familiarity
- **As little typing as possible** — Switches, selection inputs, taps and swipes wherever possible
- **No training necessary** — Most users felt comfortable within a day or two
- **Fast** — Site optimized so users aren't waiting around

### Focus 2: Process similar to current flow

- **Incoming Shipment** — The most important page. After observing the unloading process and interviewing managers, simplified into three sections. For a minimal submission, a manager could add an entire incoming shipment in a couple of minutes.
- **"Add to Cart" experience for Outgoing** — Visualized outgoing shipment as "Add to Cart" from e-commerce. Manager can "pick" stock from the list and Add to Truck. Avoids tedious forms.

### Focus 3: Data security

- **User Roles** — Traditional roles with exact permissions. Owner can restrict manager to only create records while blocking edits/deletes.
- **Vehicle Logs** — Simplest page — loads fast, one input field for vehicle number, one button for "vehicle left."

## Launch & Future

- **Quick launch** — MVP in ~3 months with proactive customer feedback
- **Lots of iterations** — Changes released 2-3 times per week with continuous feedback
- **Future:** Customisation of terminology per customer, flexible billing models (per-day, lease model)
